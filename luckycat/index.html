Lucky Cat

An album of cover versions by Glynn Bird.

A cover version usually tries to bring something new to the original, or in the case of a tribute band, an exact replica is preferred. In this album some songs have been tranformed, others are more like a photocopy of a photocopy.

The rules; only one song per artist is allowed, few drums, no guitar solos and very little post-production magic. In the end the album ended up sounding like Radio 2 sounded to me in the late 80s when I used to get a lift to work with my middle-aged colleagues.

Vocals, guitars, bass and keyboards performed by Glynn Bird.
Engineered and produced by Glynn Bird.
Recorded at home in 2019.

Track information

Original Artist: Bruce Springsteen
Written By: Bruce Springsteen
Year of Release: 2014
This is a little-known song from an obscure Bruce Springsteen E.P. I don't actually like the original track but there is a melody in there somewhere. I decided to change the time signature from 4 to 3 time and make it a lot more gentle, with some Wilson Phillips harmonies.

Original Artist: Elliott Smith
Written By: Elliott Smith
Year of Release: 1997
Elliott Smith was on repeat on my Minidisc player in the 2000s. This is a straight cover with only the key changed to keep it in range: double-tracked vocals, two acoustic guitars and an electric warble.

Original Artist: The Beatles
Written By: Lennon/McCartney
Year of Release: 1968
The White Album is one of my favourite Beatles albums and Paul McCartney to me is the king of melody. I could have picked Martha My Dear or I Will but Blackbird is easier.

Original Artist: The Cure
Written By: Smith/Gallup/Thompson/Tolhurst/Williams
Year of Release: 1987
Not a goth myself, but The Cure's Disintegration album is a stone cold classic. This track comes from the preceding album and I had the single on cassette back in the day. This cover didn't quite turn out as I expected: it seems to have become a Belle & Sebastian song.

Original Artist: Stephen Duffy
Written By: Stephen Duffy
Year of Release: 1998
Stephen Duffy, once in Duran Duran, once "Tin Tin" and then in The Lilac Time is a singer/songwriter who managed to almost completely dodge success in his forty year career. This is a b-side from a solo single that I'd forgotten about and rediscovered when one of his albums was rereleased.

Original Artist: Leonard Cohen
Written By: Leonard Cohen
Year of Release: 1967
I don't know much about Leonard Cohen, but I had his Greatest Hits cassette which I played to death and I couldn't get into the other albums as much.

Original Artist: Crowded House
Written By: Neil Finn
Year of Release: 1996
Neil Finn is like a Kiwi Paul McCartney who writes delicious melodies. Apparently this song was Finn trying to sound like the Beatles. So this is me trying to sound like Finn trying to sound like the Beatles.

Original Artist: The Smiths
Written By: Morrissey/Marr
Year of Release: 1984
A Smiths b-side and Hat Full Of Hollow favourite. The original cannot be bettered but that didn't stop me. Here it's switched from 6/8 to four time for no good reason other than to make it different.

Original Artist: U2
Written By: U2
Year of Release: 1984
One of my favourite U2 songs. It's melody works round the angular chords in the chorus and here is stripped back, Elliott Smith style, with double-tracked vocals and acoustic guitar.

Original Artist: The Leisure Society
Written By: Nick Hemming
Year of Release: 2009
I saw The Leisure Society at the Westgarth Social Club where they were promoting their first album. This song won an Ivor Novello award no less.
